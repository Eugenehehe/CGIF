Scenario: SaaS / software implementation cost treatment

A company receives invoices for a software implementation project. Line items include configuration, data migration, training, support, customization, and subscription fees. The accounting team must decide whether each item is capitalized, expensed, deferred, or escalated for technical accounting review.

Problem

Invoice descriptions are often vague. Evidence is scattered across contracts, SOWs, project plans, approvals, and vendor invoices.

Jiayi's point

Companies may need accounting judgment space. The system should not lock them into one rigid conclusion before review.

CGIF answer

Show evidence support, missing documents, risk, reviewer routing, and audit receipt instead of pretending the answer is always black-and-white.

Before CGIF-Accounting

  1. Accounting receives invoices and contracts.
  2. Staff manually reads line descriptions.
  3. Items are classified in Excel.
  4. Ambiguous cases are discussed over email/chat.
  5. Evidence is scattered across SOW, contract, PO, approval, and project records.
  6. Audit support may need to be recreated later.

After CGIF-Accounting

  1. Invoice/project evidence is entered or uploaded.
  2. CGIF classifies evidence strength and risk.
  3. CGIF lists missing support and do-not-conclude guardrails.
  4. Judgment-heavy items are routed to accounting manager, technical accounting, controller, or project owner.
  5. Reviewer approves, overrides, or requests evidence.
  6. Audit-ready receipt is preserved.
